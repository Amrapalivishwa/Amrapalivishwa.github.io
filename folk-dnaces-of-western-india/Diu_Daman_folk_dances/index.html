DIU-DAMAN - India

DIU-DAMAN FOLK DANCES

Major population is Hindu. Kokana, Varlies, Koli, Dhodia, Kathodi, Naika and Dublas are some of the major tribal community belonging to Dadra and Nagar Haveli. Their culture differ from one another.Every happy or sad occasion, like marriage or death or any harvest of crops of these tribal communities are associated with songs and dance. Other main religions are Muslim, Christian. Gujarat and Mahatashtre are neighbouring states so their culrure is also one of the factor of influence. Thus people speak Gujarati, Hindi, Marathi, Konkani languages. Main festivals are Barash (Diwali), Divaso, Bhawada, Akha Tij, Raksha Bandhan, Tarpa Festival, Craft Mela, Monsoon Magic Festival and Children’s Film Festival. The man wear dhoti up to the knees and a waist coat or shirt and a turban. The women folk wear traditional colorful saris that are either knee length or full length. Their art, craft and weaving is famous. The major occupation of the people is agriculture, fishing and hunting worldwide. Tadi is a favourite liquor drink of the people. People are mainly vegetarian. Giving respect to elders and living in peace and harmony is a part of their culture. Dadra and Nagar Haveli dance include the
Tarpa Dance,
Bhavada Dance,
Gherria Dance,
Bohada Dance,
Dhol Dance and
Tur and Thali Dance which are perfomed on various festival like Diwali as Barash, Akha Tij, Divaso and Baleo (Raksha Bandhan), Holi.
