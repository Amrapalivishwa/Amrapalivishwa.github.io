Goan Folk Dances

Goa is a place for Fish, Fenny, food, fun and Festival. Goa is known for it's Carnival and sea beaches. We visited Goa in 2013, and witnessed the mix Culture of Hindu and Christians as influenced by Portuguese. Goan life is very relaxed and rejuvenating because of it's mixed and unique culture. Main occupation is fishing and farming. There is a famous story about a goan fisher man's siasta time(afternoon resttime). They will not exchange this time for any amount of money. There are relagious, ceremonial or social and harvest festival dances throughout the year which recreates vital life force and reduces stress.

Dashavtara Dance

Dashavatar is the most developed theatre form of the Konkan and Goa regions. The performers personify the ten incarnations of Lord Vishnu-the god of preservation and creativity. The ten incarnations are Matsya (fish), Kurma (tortoise), Varaha (boar), Narsimha (lion-man), Vaman (dwarf), Parashuram, Rama, Krishna (or Balram), Buddha and Kalki. Apart from stylized make-up, the Dashavatar performers wear masks of wood and papier mache. ('Das' means ten, 'Avatar' means incarnations).

kaalo-Dance

Kaalo is yet another form of folk-play which has a deep influence of the neighbouring Karnataka folk art. The performers play with masks on, with percussionists beating at full force. The major attraction of the Kaalo is the dialogues that are humorous and ex-tempore. Even the character of Ganesha is subjected to irreverence wherein the character of priest would tell Ganesha that this pooja is being conducted by robbing and cheating the people. The pun and sarcasm amply used, is thoroughly enjoyed by the public in audience as they hear them sing of the grandchildren of a family crying because their grand-ma is not dying. Several types of Kaalo are performed in different parts of Goa. Sankasur Kaalo is named after the demon Sankasur while Gavlan Kaalo and Dhaim Kaalo are called so after the Lord Krishna. Besides these Dashavtari Kaalo, Raat Kaalo and Khell Kaalo are the other versions of Kaalo performed in different parts of Goa.

MORULO (Peacock dance) or Morulem Dance

This form of dance is closely associated with the pastoral community and hence depicts the dance of Lord Krishna and his companions. The musical accompaniment includes Pakhawaj, Zanj, Ghumat and also Harmonium. They sing songs describing the arrival of peacock in their village and its beauty. The folk dancers from Sarvan village of Bicholim taluka in north Goa district are famous for this performance.
